SWAT Analysis

Strenghts
  • Fully operational bakery, no set up required
  • Baker who started business and brought clients remains with the company
  • Immediate vertical integration to 7 Eleven business model
  • increase in GP % of fresh food items by substituting with proprietary bakery
  • Existing client based and above break even Net Income
Weaknesses
  • Expensive Price in terms of Return on Investment
  • Venture heavily relies on baker staying with the company
  • Net income should double to reach 30% per year ROI
  • 5 months of profitability makes project still uncertain
Opportunities
  • Further increase in number of clients is possible due to excess capacity
  • Business allows broad spectrum of fresh products substitution to support 7 Eleven ongoing expansion
  • Management synergies between 7 Eleven & bakery senior member

Conclusions:

Positive Remarks:
  • The purchase offers the opportunity to increase the profitability on the fresh food items category.
  • It strategically supports the expansion of 7 Eleven in the UAE.
  • The ample excess capacity allows the company to continue growing without compromising the vertical integration with 7 Eleven.
Other Remarks:
  • High purchase price: This factor is partially mitigated by avoiding the set up cost as well as the easiness of a quick integration with 7 Eleven.
  • The focus of the company should be set on keep growing the existing client base to reach a yearly ROI above 30%.
  • It is imperative to reduce the dependency on one client (Milk & Honey) to remain profitable.
Legal Recommendation:
  • All assets should be transferred to a new LLC company as well as employees.
  • All contracts with 3rd parties should be transferred to new LLC.
  • Commercial name should remain the same under the umbrella of the new LLC.
  • To transfer all assets and contracts to new LLC eliminates the risk of absorving unknown liabilites coming from existing company.
